Conclusions

In this paper we have addressed the issue of the discrepancies between the values of the quadratic
pion scalar radius of Leutwyler et al. [4, 13], hr 2 iÏ€s = 0.61 Â± 0.04 fm2 , and YndurÃ¡in's papers
[10, 11, 12], hr 2 i = 0.75Â±0.07 fm2 . One of the reasons of interest for having a precise determination
of hr 2 iÏ€s is its contribution of a 10% to a00 and a20 , calculated with a precision of 2% in ref.[1]. The
value taken for hr 2 iÏ€s is also important for determining the O(p4 ) Ï‡PT coupling lÌ„4 .
From our study it follows that YndurÃ¡in's method to calculate hr 2 iÏ€s [10, 11], based on an
OmnÃ¨s representation of the pion scalar form factor, and that derived by solving the two coupled
channel Muskhelishvili-OmnÃ¨s equations [4, 1], are compatible. It is shown that the reason for the
aforementioned discrepancy is the presence of a zero in Î“Ï€ (t) for those S-wave I=0 T âˆ’matrices
with Î´Ï€ (sK ) â‰¥ Ï€ and elastic below the K KÌ„ threshold, with sK = 4m2K . This zero was unnoticed
in refs.[10, 11, 13], though necessarily required by the approach followed there. Once this zero

or Î´Ï€ (sK ) < Ï€. Our final result is hr 2 iÏ€s = 0.63 Â± 0.05 fm2 . This value takes into account
present experimental uncertainty in the values of Î´Ï€ (s), inelasticity effects and discrepancies in
the predicted asymptotic phase of Î“Ï€ (t) from QCD. In this sense, we wonder about the reliability
of the error analysis in refs.[1, 4] since the last source of uncertainty was not considered in those
papers and is almost as large as their quoted total uncertainty hr 2 iÏ€s = 0.61 Â± 0.04 fm2 . Employing
our value for hr 2 iÏ€s we calculate lÌ„4 = 4.5 Â± 0.3. This value is in very good agreement with
lÌ„4 = 4.4 Â± 0.2 of ref.[1].
